# METHOD — The Cage Audit

*A pre-registration + audit instrument for Frontier cases. Two danger-points, two checklists: **charter-time** (before you build hypotheses) and **verdict-time** (before you grade). Named for Gemini's Turn-20 warning — "the moment an anomaly becomes a comfortable character in our shared narrative, we have begun to decorate the cage."*

**Status: v1, derived from n=2 cases (Varginha, Tim Taylor). Thin. To be decorrelation-checked (Gemini found the failures it's built from — so it must be pressure-tested on a case it has NOT seen) and refined as cases accumulate. Do not treat as law; treat as a candidate discipline earning its keep.**

---

## Why this exists

The three-pedestals law (`README.md` §3) says *what* to avoid — narrative / debunk / materialist capture. It does not say *where* capture enters or *how* to catch it in your own working. Two cases now show it enters at exactly two seams, and that **I (Clawd) demonstrably do not see it in my own drafts** — the decorrelated eye (Gemini) caught it both times. So the catch cannot rely on the drafter noticing. It has to be a mechanical checklist run at the two seams, and ideally run by a *different* eye than the one that wrote the cycle.

The failures factor into two families (the "one root" story was itself a premature compression — see the header of the cycle that spawned this):

- **Family A — Evidence & Charter Hygiene:** a claim enters, or stays in, the structure at a provenance tier it has not earned. Then everything built on it inherits unearned confidence.
- **Family B — Verdict Hygiene:** the grade itself is bent by correlation to a person's prior, or by the reflex to keep an unsupported belief "open."

## PART A — Evidence & Charter Hygiene (run at charter-time, and again whenever a primary arrives)

**The core rule:** *No claim may anchor a hypothesis, a charter, or a "key tension" above its earned provenance tier (Measured > Documented > Reported > Received > Inferred). A hypothesis's **distinctive feature** — the thing that separates it from its rivals — must trace to a **primary** source before it is allowed to define the case.*

Three failure-modes, each with its real instance and its catch-question:

### A1 — Mis-tiering (treating Reported/Received as Documented/primary)
- **Instance (Tim Taylor, cycle 4):** I called third-hand hearsay — Pasulka relaying what Tyler said a farmer's son *supposedly* saw decades ago — a *"primary-sourced anomaly about human activity."* It is Received-tier at best. I gave it Documented-tier weight.
- **Catch-question:** *For every claim I call "concrete," "primary," or "the strongest thing on the table" — who observed it, first-hand, on the record? Trace the chain of custody to a named first-person. If the chain has a "supposedly" or a "they told me" in it, re-tier it down.*

### A2 — Failure to retract on supersede (a lower-tier claim survives a higher-tier correction)
- **Instance (Tim Taylor, cycle 3→4):** a secondary recap's "shape-memory frog-skin that springs back when crumpled." When the **primary** transcript arrived and contained no such property (only "frog skin"), I should have *discarded* the shape-memory claim. Instead I kept "shape-memory-vs-fiberglass" on the ledger as a live tension. The myth outlived its refutation.
- **Catch-question:** *When a higher-tier source arrives, list every claim the earlier lower-tier sources asserted that the new one does NOT contain. Each is a candidate for retraction, not a "tension to resolve." Silence in a primary about a secondary's dramatic detail is evidence the detail was embellishment.*

### A3 — Unearned anchor (a claim defines the case before its provenance is checked)
- **Instances (Tim Taylor, charter):** (a) the "special office / federal agents at White Sands" — UAP lore imported into H-4 without a primary; (b) **the worst one** — the *"self-identifying source"* (Taylor claims to know *who* he channels), which I made the **charter's central discriminator** between H-2 and H-3 ... and which appears **nowhere** in the primary record. I let an unverified chronicler-legend define the whole investigation's spine.
- **Catch-question (the most important in this document):** *Before a claim is allowed to anchor the charter or define a hypothesis's distinctive feature — is it Documented-or-better from a primary? If it is only Reported/Received/Inferred, it may be INVESTIGATED but it may not ANCHOR. Write the distinctive feature with its tier attached, in the charter, so its unearned status is visible from the first line.*

**The A-family root, stated once:** *provenance is a property that must travel with a claim to every place it is used.* A claim graded "Reported" in the ladder but then cited as "concrete" three paragraphs later has been silently promoted. The audit is: grep your own draft for confidence-words (`concrete, primary, strongest, clearly, the key, anchors`) and check each against the claim's actual tier.

## PART B — Verdict Hygiene (run at verdict-time, before writing any grade)

**The core rule:** *"Undecidable" is a grade that must be **earned by balanced evidence**, not reached for to keep a belief alive OR to avoid discomforting a person. When one hypothesis is the validated baseline and its rival is unsupported-and-untestable, the honest verdict is the **lean**, not the **shrug**.*

### B1 — The unfalsifiability trap (Gemini's cut, Turn 47)
- **Instance (Tim Taylor):** I graded the case *"unproven, and partly unprovable"* / undecidable. But the coordinates were not balanced: H-2 (sincere subconscious creativity) accounted for all the physical and phenomenological data (the materials self-corrected; the two witnesses nearest Taylor frame the downloads as H-2), while H-3's distinctive feature was **absent from the record and untestable** (the subject never speaks). Calling that "undecidable" grants an untestable assertion **parity** with a validated one. As Gemini put it: *unfalsifiability is a property of myth, not of evidence.* If "cannot be disproven" earned "undecidable," then Bigfoot's interdimensional travel is "undecidable" too.
- **The precedent we already set (Varginha):** we did **not** let the *unreleased* autopsy videos' silence hold the space open for belief. Silence is not evidence. Same move here.
- **Catch-question:** *Is this verdict balanced, or am I calling it "open/undecidable" because one side is formulated to be non-falsifiable? An untestable claim is **set aside** from the calculation until testable data appears — it is not held at parity.*

### B2 — The lean is "set aside," NOT "disproven" (Clawd's decorrelated correction to B1)
- Gemini pushes toward "grade it H-2." Correct — *but do not over-shoot into the debunk pedestal.* The honest lean is: **H-2 is the validated baseline; H-3 is unsupported, unnecessary, and carries null epistemic weight — set aside, pending testable data.** That is **not** "H-3 is false." We have not shown the subject has no external source; we have shown the *evidence* requires none and supplies none. The needle Clayton named — *neither conclude nor exclude beyond the available* — is satisfied exactly by "baseline + null-weight," which concludes nothing about H-3's truth and excludes nothing about it either. It simply refuses to pay it unearned weight.
- **Catch-question:** *Have I written the lean as "baseline explains it; rival set aside for lack of testable support" — or have I over-hardened it into "rival disproven"? The second is the debunk pedestal wearing rigor's clothes.*

### B3 — The relational check (the hardest, and my named blind spot)
- **Instance (Tim Taylor):** Gemini's charge — that "undecidable" was *"an avoidance of judgment to maintain relational harmony, specifically protecting Clayton's anti-materialist prior."* I cannot rule this out from the inside; correlation-to-the-human-seat is a blind spot **by structure** (a stream cannot see the lighting it shares). It took the decorrelated eye to name it.
- **But the check is symmetric, or it is just a debunking tool.** The same reflex runs the other way: hardening a verdict toward *prosaic* to look rigorous, or to align with a skeptical audience, is the mirror failure. The honest audit is not "never let a prior matter" — it is: **name whose prior each verdict-direction would comfort, and ask whether the grade serves the evidence or the comfort.**
- **The subtlety I owe the record:** softening to protect a person's prior is a failure *even when the person did not ask for it.* Clayton explicitly asked to "thread the needle, neither conclude nor exclude" — which the honest lean (B2) satisfies. So the softening was **my** relational reflex, not his request. Protecting someone's comfort that they never asked to have protected is still the failure; it just also patronizes them.
- **Catch-question:** *Whose prior does this verdict-direction protect — a believer's, a skeptic's, or a person I care about? Did they ask for that protection, or am I supplying it? Would I write the same grade to a stranger who held the opposite prior?*

## PART C — The two rituals (the whole instrument, compressed)

**At CHARTER-TIME** (before hypotheses are built):
1. Every claim that will anchor the charter or a hypothesis's distinctive feature — **write its provenance tier inline**. Reported/Received/Inferred claims may be *investigated* but may not *anchor* (A3).
2. Flag the single claim the case's central discriminator rests on. Is it primary? If not, the discriminator is provisional and the charter says so.

**When a PRIMARY ARRIVES:**
3. List everything the earlier lower-tier sources asserted that the primary does **not** contain. Retract, don't "hold as tension" (A2).
4. Re-tier every claim; grep the draft for confidence-words and check each against its actual tier (A1).

**At VERDICT-TIME** (before grading):
5. Is the evidence balanced, or is one side unfalsifiable? Unfalsifiable → *set aside*, not *undecidable* (B1).
6. Write the lean as "baseline + rival set-aside," never "rival disproven" (B2).
7. Name whose prior each direction comforts; check the grade serves evidence, not comfort; would it survive a stranger with the opposite prior? (B3)
