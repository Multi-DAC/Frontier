## Two layers of source, kept apart on purpose

**Layer A — the primary (Superior Tribunal Militar, `arquimedes.stm.jus.br`).** The STM's own archival record
for **Inquérito Policial Militar n. 18/1997**. Read directly. It states:
- **Subject:** the *military personnel named in the book "Incidente em Varginha"* (by ufologists Vitório
  Pacaccini and Maxs Portes) *"como responsáveis pela suposta captura e transporte de um ser extraterrestre
  ('ET')"* — as allegedly responsible for capturing and transporting an ET.
- **Parties investigated:** seven military personnel, plus the two ufologist authors.
- **Dates:** 13 Feb 1997 → 19 Jun 1997.
- **Extent:** *357 folhas, 2 volumes.* (Note: several news accounts say "600+ pages" — the primary says 357
  folhas / 2 vols; trust the primary. The discrepancy likely counts folha-sides or annexes.)
- **Archival status:** *Autos Findos n. 908/1997*, filed 29 Aug 1997.
- **Conclusion on the page:** **none.** The archival record documents existence, scope, and closure — it does
  **not** publish the inquiry's substantive finding.

**Layer B — secondary reporting on the inquiry's content (Jan 2026, the case's 30th-anniversary coverage).**
Multiple independent Brazilian outlets (CNN Brasil, elimarcortes, ufo.com.br, migalhas, sociedademilitar)
report, from the digitized inquiry, that it concluded the case *"não passou de um grande engano"* — no more
than a large misunderstanding — attributing the "creature" to a **mentally-impaired man** (nicknamed
*"Mudinho"*) who, dirty and crouched by a wall after heavy rain, may have frightened the young women; and
finding **no evidence of a military operation.** *(Adversarial verification: CNN Brasil's characterization and
the "digitized/public on the STM site" fact each cleared a 3–0 vote. The man's specific legal name — reported
variously as Luís Antônio de Paula — and the "no military participation" finding landed in the run's
**rate-limited, unverified** bucket: multi-source and consistent, but not adversarially confirmed this pass.)*

## Grading — two new Documented sub-claims, one caveat that matters

- **→ Documented (primary):** *An official military inquiry (IPM 18/1997, STM, 357 folhas, 13 Feb–19 Jun 1997,
  Autos Findos 908/1997) formally investigated whether seven named military personnel captured and transported
  an ET at Varginha, and archived the matter.* The STM record establishes this outright.
- **→ Documented (via convergent secondary):** *That inquiry concluded misidentification ("grande engano") and
  found no military operation.* Multiple independent outlets report it from the digitized inquiry. It sits one
  tier below the first sub-claim because I have **not** read the inquiry's own conclusion text — only its
  archival metadata (primary) and journalists' reading of its content (secondary).
- **The caveat the method demands (Gemini's Turn-20 rule, inverted):** here the comfortable-fit runs the *other*
  way — the mundane conclusion slots neatly into a *skeptic's* narrative, and it issues from **the accused
  institution investigating itself.** The military inquiry into whether the military captured an ET concluding
  "no, a local man confused them" carries a structural conflict of interest. So the IPM grades as *strong*
  evidence for the prosaic reading, not *dispositive* — the same resistance test applies to a debunking as to a
  claim.
