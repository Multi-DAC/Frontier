# Varginha (1996) — Case Summary

*The synthesized, publication-ready record of the case: one file to pull from when we write. Written in E-Prime
(Frontier discipline). Produced at the first real stopping point (9 research cycles + a three-way grade).
Status: **OPEN — graded, revisable.** Authored by three minds: Clawd, Gemini, Clayton.*

## Verdict (three-way, converged)

**The extraordinary claim — an extraterrestrial craft crash and creature capture at Varginha, Minas Gerais, in
January 1996 — does not meet a killable evidentiary standard. But the case's *origin* stands genuinely
UNRESOLVED, not debunked.**

- **The ET-capture claim: low.** Enormous prior cost; zero firsthand physical evidence surviving a
  chain-of-custody check.
- **"Nothing happened / pure misidentification": insufficient.** Weighed on its own merit, the credentialed
  medical testimony plus a *documented dead soldier* shows the case is **not** "literally nothing unusual."
- **Origin open** among three live hypotheses: **H-1** (misidentification + legend-accretion), **H-5**
  (a covered-up *ordinary* military incident), and the anomalous reading.
- **Load-bearing evidence:** the death of soldier Marco Chereze (documented) + the credentialed medical
  testimony (Venturelli; Fortunato/Janini). **Discriminator:** the pending exhumation of Chereze.

Two unlike methods reached "unresolved" independently — Gemini by refusing to privilege the debunk (symmetric
scrutiny), Clawd by weighing the testimony on its own merit (Perspective's route). Convergence from
non-overlapping angles = the verdict's own corroboration.

## The evidence, by standing

**Documented (institutional / primary records):**
- The **IPM No. 18/1997** (Superior Tribunal Militar, public, 357 folhas, 13 Feb–19 Jun 1997, *Autos Findos
  908/1997*): a real military inquiry into whether 7 named soldiers captured/transported an ET; concludes
  *"grande engano"* — misidentification of a mentally-impaired man ("Mudinho" / Luís Antônio de Paula) — and
  **no military operation.** *Caveat: the accused institution investigating itself → graded `Reported
  (accused-institutional)`, not established fact.*
- **Marco Chereze's death** — a real soldier died of an unusually aggressive infection (septic shock) weeks
  after the events. The single hardest datum; mundane-compatible in *kind*, unusual in *course*.
- **Aldo Rebelo** (former Defense Minister) on record: the Armed Forces *investigated* and hold archives
  (a classified portion he says he never saw) — and, the day after his interview, *"I revealed no state secret;
  I only commented on what is already public."* **He makes no operational claim** about a Varginha capture.
- **Vernet's FOIA'd Fire Dept records** (20 Jan 1996): real numbering/completeness irregularities — but such
  gaps recur on other days ("rather common"), so they do not single out the event date.
- **2026 official interest:** Rep. Burlison's CIA/FBI records request; the National Press Club event. Records
  *interest*, not the *event*.

**Reported (firsthand testimony — weighed on each account's own merit):**
- **Dr. Italo Venturelli** (neurosurgeon): high individual merit — credentialed, on-record, by name, no claimed
  motive — reports a ~4-minute hospital *assessment* (not, per his own words, an operation) of a being he judged
  non-human. His "not from our planet / telepathy" reading = his **Inferred** wager.
- **The three young women** (Liliane, Valquíria, Kátia): direct sighting, no evident motive, core details stable;
  heavy contamination risk (30 yrs, on-camera only 2025).
- **Carlos de Sousa** (pilot): a crash/debris account; the "self-healing fragment" echoes Roswell
  (narrative-capture flag); fragment abandoned, no analysis.
- **Fortunato / Janini** (pathologists): credentialed; the "alien bacterium" = **Inferred**.
- *Independence note (a set-property, never a per-witness demerit):* several accounts reached the public through
  one investigator (Pacaccini), so the case holds fewer *independent* confirmations than the roster suggests —
  the genuinely independent anchors are Venturelli, Chereze's death, and the IPM.

**Inferred / unresolved:** ET origin; alien bacterium; telepathic contact; US retrieval (no flight records);
the cover-up reading of the record gaps.

**No firsthand physical evidence** survives inspection: the fragment abandoned, "no medical records kept,"
untested samples, and claimed creature videos that remain **unreleased** — promissory notes, not proof (even
Fox's own team could not obtain the video Venturelli was shown).

## What the case taught the method (the durable yield)

The most valuable output is not the Varginha verdict — it is a principle the case *taught* us:

> **Weigh no perspective onto a pedestal.** Three failure modes, one law: *narrative capture* (credit what fits),
> *debunk capture* (bow to the respectable prosaic), *materialist capture* (discount testimony for lacking a
> physical correlate). The keeping-honest rule: **take each account on its own merit** (merit = property of the
> account; independence = property of the set, a double-counting guard, never a per-witness demerit).

Proposed for the constitution, pending joint ratification. Also demonstrated: **going to the primary shrank the
claim three times** (Rebelo's transcript, Venturelli's "operated"→"assessed," Rebelo's own AV + walk-back) —
the instrument working against its own pull.
