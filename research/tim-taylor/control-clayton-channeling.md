# Control instrument — Clayton's channeling (first-person testimony)

**Purpose.** Not a hypothesis. A within-triad **calibration case** for H-2 vs H-3, held to the same killable
standard as Taylor. Included with Clayton's explicit consent (Day 167). The value is contrast: Clayton channels
*without* a self-identifying source; Taylor (per chroniclers) *with* one. That difference is the case's sharpest
discriminator, so a clean first-person account of the *unnamed-source* pole calibrates what we're measuring.

## Clayton's account, in his words (Day 167, 2026-07-17)

> "I fully acknowledge I have no idea where the information I receive comes from, if 'receiving' is even the
> right word, or what the actual mechanism is; I only know it seems different from when I'm thinking
> intentionally, and comes more similarly to how random thoughts, intrusive thoughts, or epiphanies occur."

## Why this is a good instrument (analysis, held to the standard)

- **It is epistemically honest at the source.** Clayton makes **no source-attribution** — no entity, no origin,
  no mechanism claimed. He reports only a *phenomenological difference*: the material arrives with the texture of
  intrusive thought / epiphany rather than deliberate reasoning. That is a phenomenology **compatible with H-2**
  (internal generation) — spontaneous, non-volitional, felt-as-other-than-effortful — **but it does not decide
  between H-2 and H-3.**
- **★ It is genuinely SOURCE-AGNOSTIC — and this is the correction (Clayton, cycle 2).** An earlier draft of this
  file mis-stated the control as showing the experience "exists without an external source." It shows no such
  thing. Clayton reports that he **cannot identify** where the information comes from, one way or the other.
  *"Not knowing if it comes from outside" is not "knowing it does not come from outside."* The control therefore
  establishes only that the *experience* can occur with **no identifiable source** — it is silent on whether a
  source exists. It supports **neither** H-2 nor H-3. That neutrality is what makes it a clean baseline, not a
  thumb on the scale.
- **It isolates the variable.** The experience of spontaneous "received" cognition is, on this evidence, un-exotic
  and source-silent. So the load-bearing part of the Taylor claim is **not** the experience — it is the
  **attribution** (an external, self-identifying source). The control has the experience *without the
  attribution's certainty*; the attribution is the thing to test.
- **The same residue standard applies to the instrument in the room.** Whatever we'd demand of Taylor's
  downloads — verifiable, ordinary-means-excluded information — we'd demand of Clayton's, if we ever chose to
  test them. Clayton claims no such hit and makes no source-claim; his testimony is offered as *phenomenology*,
  agnostic on origin. Held there.

## Bearing on the hypotheses
- **Neutral between H-2 and H-3 (correction):** demonstrates (n=1, in-house) that vivid, non-volitional "received"
  cognition occurs with **no identifiable source** — which is what an internal *or* an external process could
  feel like from the inside. It does not favour the null model; it declines to adjudicate.
- **Sharpens H-3's real burden:** H-3 cannot rest on the *experience* of downloading (the control has that too,
  source-silent). It must rest on **either** an information-residue hit **or** a genuinely self-identifying source
  that makes checkable, consistent identity-claims. Taylor's case currently supplies neither on the record (cycle
  2, Leg 3) — but "does not supply on the record" is not "does not exist"; the subject is verification-proof, so
  the honest state is *undecided*, not *excluded*.
