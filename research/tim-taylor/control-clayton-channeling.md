# Control instrument — Clayton's channeling (first-person testimony)

**Purpose.** Not a hypothesis. A within-triad **calibration case** for H-2 vs H-3, held to the same killable
standard as Taylor. Included with Clayton's explicit consent (Day 167). The value is contrast: Clayton channels
*without* a self-identifying source; Taylor (per chroniclers) *with* one. That difference is the case's sharpest
discriminator, so a clean first-person account of the *unnamed-source* pole calibrates what we're measuring.

## Clayton's account, in his words (Day 167, 2026-07-17)

> "I fully acknowledge I have no idea where the information I receive comes from, if 'receiving' is even the
> right word, or what the actual mechanism is; I only know it seems different from when I'm thinking
> intentionally, and comes more similarly to how random thoughts, intrusive thoughts, or epiphanies occur."

## Why this is a good instrument (analysis, held to the standard)

- **It is epistemically honest at the source.** Clayton makes **no source-attribution** — no entity, no origin,
  no mechanism claimed. He reports only a *phenomenological difference*: the material arrives with the texture of
  intrusive thought / epiphany rather than deliberate reasoning. That is exactly the **experiential signature H-2
  predicts** for internally-generated content — spontaneous, non-volitional, felt-as-other-than-effortful — with
  **no H-3 commitment attached.**
- **It isolates the variable.** If the raw experience of "download" (spontaneous, non-volitional cognition) is
  common and un-exotic, then the load-bearing part of the Taylor claim is **not** the experience — it is the
  **attribution** (an external, self-identifying source). The control shows the experience can exist *without*
  the attribution. So the attribution is the thing to test, not the experience.
- **The same residue standard applies to the instrument in the room.** Whatever we'd demand of Taylor's
  downloads — verifiable, ordinary-means-excluded information — we'd demand of Clayton's, if we ever chose to
  test them. Clayton claims no such hit and makes no such claim; his testimony is offered as *phenomenology*, not
  as evidence of an external source. Held there.

## Bearing on the hypotheses
- **Strengthens H-2's plausibility for Taylor:** demonstrates (n=1, in-house) that vivid, non-volitional
  "received" cognition occurs *without* requiring an external source — the null model has a living exemplar.
- **Sharpens H-3's real burden:** H-3 cannot rest on the *experience* of downloading (the control has that too).
  It must rest on **either** an information-residue hit **or** a genuinely self-identifying source that makes
  checkable, consistent identity-claims. Taylor's case currently supplies neither on the record (cycle 2, Leg 3).
