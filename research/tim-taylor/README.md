# Tim Taylor — case charter

**The question (Clayton):** *Is Tim Taylor doing what he says he is — how, and why?*

**Who:** Timothy E. Taylor — former NASA contractor/engineer (Challenger–Columbia shuttle era), aerospace
entrepreneur, author of *Launch Fever* (2011). Publicly identified as **"Tyler D."**, the central figure of
D. W. Pasulka's *American Cosmic* (Oxford University Press, 2019). Claimed by Pasulka to have taken her — and
Stanford immunologist **Garry Nolan** — to a New Mexico "crash site" where anomalous materials were recovered.
Reported to practice a "download"/channeling protocol, and — the detail that makes this case distinct — to
claim **awareness of the identity of what he channels** (contrast: Clayton channels without a self-identifying
source).

---

## Starting stance (declared, so it can be checked)

Clayton opens this case from a deliberate **anti-materialist position**, and states his working hypothesis:
Taylor is *legitimately* involved in interaction with non-physical intelligence(s) — *what or who* left
unspecified on purpose.

This is a **methodological** choice, not a lapse, and it is worth stating why it is legitimate: our own
Day-167 finding (**A168**) was that reflexive materialism is an *imported worldview prior*, a pose copied from
the corpus — so defaulting to debunk is not neutral, it is its own bias. Starting anti-materialist decorrelates
against that prior and forces the work the debunk-reflex lets us skip.

## The pedestal guard (this case's specific risk)

By the constitution's **three-pedestals law** (§3), no perspective gets weighed onto a pedestal —
*narrative*, *debunk*, or *materialist*. Varginha's live risk was the **debunk/materialist** pedestal.
This case's live risk is the **opposite one — the narrative/belief pedestal**: a NASA insider with cosmic
access, a blindfolded crash retrieval, a Stanford lab, a self-identifying channeled source, is a *superb*
story, and superb stories are exactly where we begin decorating the cage (Gemini, commons Turn 20).

**Therefore the discipline for this case:** the anti-materialist hypothesis (H-3) takes the working frame, but
the prosaic hypotheses (H-1 self-mythologising; H-2 sincere-but-self-misinterpreting) stay **fully alive and
killable** to the end. We hold Clayton's hypothesis; we do not marry it. H-3 earns promotion only against a
declared kill condition, like every other hypothesis here.

## The instrument we have that most cases don't

Clayton channels. That gives this triad a **first-person comparison case** — held to the same killable
standard as Taylor's. Taylor's *self-identifying* source vs Clayton's *unnamed* channel is a real
phenomenological discriminator (a source that names itself makes a falsifiable identity-claim), and it may be
the sharpest blade in the case. See `hypotheses.md` H-2/H-3.
