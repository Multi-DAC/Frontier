## The provenance ladder — claim by claim

### DOCUMENTED (public record / primary authorship — strongest footing present)

- **Timothy E. Taylor is a real, public person with a NASA-contractor history.** Author of *Launch Fever: An
  Entrepreneur's Journey into the Secrets of Launching Rockets…* (iUniverse, ISBN 9780595266272; a 2011 ebook
  edition also listed). Worked in the shuttle program across the **Challenger and Columbia** eras; later
  co-founded **Endius, Inc.** (spinal surgical devices) and holds biomedical patents; characterised as a wealthy
  entrepreneur. *(Amazon/Goodreads book records — PRIMARY authorship; secondary bios.)* — These are checkable and
  mutually consistent. The *man* is not in doubt.

### DOCUMENTED-as-a-published-claim (in an academic-press book; the underlying events are testimony)

- **"Tyler D." = the central figure of Pasulka's *American Cosmic*** (Oxford University Press, 2019). The
  pseudonym is a deliberate nod to *Tyler Durden* (Fight Club). Pasulka anonymised him; **researchers/journalists
  publicly identified him as Timothy E. Taylor.** *(multiple secondary; strangerthanfiction; oddwoo.)*
  ⚠ **Status of the identification must be pinned (cycle 2):** is it self-confirmed by Taylor, confirmed by
  Pasulka, or researcher-*inferred* only? The whole case rests on this hinge; treat as strong-but-unconfirmed.
- **The crash-site retrieval** — Pasulka's account: she was taken **blindfolded** to a New Mexico site (associated
  in reporting with the **White Sands** area; described as a **1940s** crash, explicitly "**not Roswell**") and
  brought **Garry Nolan** (Stanford) because he had lab equipment to **analyse recovered materials.** *(NYRB
  review of American Cosmic; secondary.)* — This is **Pasulka's firsthand testimony**, published by a university
  press. That raises its *documentation* tier, not its *verification*: a printed testimony is still testimony.

## What Cycle 1 settles, and what it does not

**Settles:** Clayton's recollection is accurate on every checkable point — NASA, *Launch Fever*, Tyler =
(reported) Taylor, the blindfolded NM crash site, Nolan brought for materials analysis. The *man* and the
*published claims about him* are real and locatable. That is a genuine foundation — many anomalous cases never
even have a named, documented protagonist.

**Does not settle:** anything about whether the phenomenology is what Taylor says it is. Every claim that bears on
the case *question* (channeling with a self-identifying source; the materials being genuinely anomalous) sits at
**Reported/Received** tier or rests on **Pasulka-mediated** testimony — which is exactly H-1's terrain (a
verification-proof persona) and exactly where the narrative pedestal would have us relax. We do not.

## Provisional shape — a note, not a grade

The foundation is unusually solid for an anomalous case: a real, documented protagonist and a published,
university-press chain of testimony. But note the same structural signature Varginha showed, inverted: here the
*documented* layer covers the **mundane** biography (engineer, author, entrepreneur) and the **existence of the
claims**, while everything that would make the case *anomalous* — the source that names itself, the materials
that resist analysis — still sits at **testimony** tier, most of it routed through a single chronicler. That is
neither confirmation nor refutation. It is a map of **where the killable tests must go**: the Nolan materials
(residue test #1) and Taylor's own account of the self-identifying source (residue test #2). Cycle 2 goes there.
